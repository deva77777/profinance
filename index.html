Mastering Mortgage Planning with CalcWealth

How Mortgage Interest is Calculated

In the 2026 economic landscape, most mortgage lenders use the Reducing Balance Method. Interest is calculated on the remaining principal amount every month. This means as you pay off your loan, the interest portion of your EMI decreases while the principal repayment portion increases.

Expert Tip: Refinance Early

If market rates drop by more than 0.75%, consider refinancing to save thousands over the loan life.

Did you know?

Bi-weekly payments can shave up to 4 years off a 30-year mortgage.

Why SIP is the Wealth Engine of 2026

The Magic of Compounding

Systematic Investment Plans (SIPs) utilize Dollar-Cost Averaging. By investing a fixed amount regularly, you buy more units when prices are low and fewer when prices are high.

Glossary: Compounding Frequency

In 2026, most SIPs compound monthly. This means your interest earns interest every single month, accelerating your wealth growth exponentially compared to annual compounding.

SIP Best Practices

  • Start Early: Investing at age 25 vs 35 can triple your final corpus.
  • Automate: Set up a standing instruction to avoid missing cycles.
  • Step-Up SIP: Increase your monthly investment by 10% every year as your salary grows.

Retirement Planning in the 2026 Economy

The Impact of Inflation

When planning for retirement, the biggest silent threat is inflation. A monthly expense of $2,000 today could easily double in 15-20 years. Our calculator uses the 4% Withdrawal Rule to estimate how much you need to never run out of money.

Golden Rules for Retirement

  • Account for Healthcare: Medical costs often rise faster than general inflation.
  • Diversify Assets: Don't rely solely on one pension or stock market index.
  • Stay Invested: Even in retirement, a portion of your corpus should stay in growth assets to beat inflation.

GST and VAT Compliance 2026

Understanding Indirect Taxes

Whether you're a business owner or a consumer, calculating the tax component of a transaction is vital for budgeting. GST (Goods and Services Tax) or VAT (Value Added Tax) is typically added on top of the base price.

Pro Tip: Inclusive vs Exclusive Tax

Our tool calculates the tax on top of the base price (Exclusive). To find the base price from a total price (Inclusive), use the formula: Base = Total / (1 + Tax Rate).

Financial Glossary

Amortization
The process of gradually paying off a debt over time through regular installments of principal and interest.
Compound Interest
Interest calculated on the initial principal, which also includes all of the accumulated interest from previous periods.
Net Worth
The total value of everything you own (assets) minus everything you owe (liabilities).
Yield
The earnings generated and realized on an investment over a particular period of time.
